# CAD-ING The Gearboxes!

### **Today I started designing the gearboxes that will drive the joints of my Supernumerary Robotic Arms.**

#### Since I want my arms to have the ability to pick up items tha weigh more than 4 pounds, I decided that I should make gearboxes for every joint. We are going to prototype our design and 3D Print them. Then once we come to a final design, we will mill them out.

Our To-Do list consists of:
- Modeling the gearbox housing to securely hold the servo  
- Aligning the output shaft with the ring gear  
- Planning the gear stages to achieve the torque multiplication needed for smooth and precise motion  
- Making the design modular so gearboxes can be swapped or upgraded later  

#### *The goal is to have a fully functional mechanical system that is ready for prototyping.*

---

# Design Process

This Cad session is dedicated to making a gearbox that sits well witht the other part, as well as being compact and light.

#### Key Steps Taken

- Modeled servo mounting points and housing shape  
- Designed gear stages to increase torque without sacrificing speed  
- Checked for alignment and interference between gears  
- Verified that tolerances are reasonable for 3D printing  

I also made sure to account for weight distribution so the gearbox doesn’t unbalance the arm when mounted on the back frame.

---

# Potential Challenges

Predictiong some challeges will be crucial during this step because if the gearbox dosen't work, the rest of the arm won't either. Some challenges I anticipate include:

**1. Gear Alignment & Tolerances**  
- Precise alignment is crucial for smooth motion  
- Misalignment could cause binding or premature wear  

**2. Torque & Load Capacity**  
- Calculating the correct gear ratio to amplify servo torque while keeping smooth motion  
- Ensuring the gearbox can handle forces during stabilization tasks  

**3. Modularity & Integration**  
- Designing a housing that is easy to attach/detach from the arm  
- Ensuring the gearbox fits within the back-mounted frame without interfering with natural movement  

---

# Next Steps

- Refine tolerances and clearances in CAD  
- Simulate movement under load to check for stress points  
- Begin 3D printing prototype components  
- Test fit and alignment before assembling full arm
